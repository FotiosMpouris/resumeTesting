What's Running Right Now

LIVE

OpenCare — Built Because I Needed It

Built by a caregiver, not by a startup that interviewed caregivers. OpenCare started as my attempt to stop drowning in group texts while managing my parents' care from across state lines. It's the opening chapter of something larger: a decentralized human-services marketplace where AI agents replace the 95% of bureaucratic work that is purely procedural, and human Sentinels handle the 5% that requires real judgment. Identity on Nostr. Coordination currency that isn't speculation. Infrastructure for the people who can't afford to wait for the system to work.

53M unpaid US family caregivers · ~$30/mo server cost · Running on one EC2 instance.

opencare.poorpeople.app  ·  poorpeople.app
PRODUCTION

Hard-E — The Sales Director That Picks Up Every Time

A contractor friend kept losing leads while he was on a job site, hands dirty, unable to answer his phone. Hard-E sits inside his CRM, knowledge base, and pricing sheets. Claude thinks. Cartesia speaks in 40 milliseconds. Deepgram listens. Twenty-one tools cover the full contractor workflow from customer lookup to signed proposal. It's not a voice assistant. It's a coworker who never misses a follow-up because it never gets tired, distracted, or busy on a roof.

~300 API calls/day · 99.7% success rate · Multi-tenant.

harde.app
TECHNICAL LEAD

All Angles — AI That Earns Its Keep

A 30-year remodeling company deserved better than a spreadsheet. Now a lead arrives enriched, not raw. Someone photographs a house. GPT-4o vision reads the exterior — siding material, condition, style. Perplexity researches the property. The enriched lead hits DynamoDB and a notification email before a human has picked up a pen. The company still runs on reputation. The pipeline just makes sure the reputation has more chances to do its work.

James Hardie Elite Preferred · Trusted since 1997 · AI-enriched pipeline live.

aaexterior.com
24/7 AUTONOMOUS

Ethos — My 24/7 AI Chief of Staff

I needed someone to coordinate all of this while I slept. Ethos is a Hermes Agent running on Lightsail, communicating via Telegram. It manages real care tasks through the OpenCare API, runs a 9-lens video intelligence pipeline, orchestrates an autonomous art pipeline from S3 to Instagram, and maintains a 105-page knowledge wiki that knows more about my projects than I can hold in my head at once. It costs less than a coffee per day. $2–4. That number still makes me smile.

$2–4/day · 105 wiki pages · Claude Sonnet 4.6 + Haiku 4.5.

How I Got Here

I came into this sideways — restaurant, art school, Unity game dev, then Python, then the agents hit and everything accelerated. I don't have a computer science degree. What I have is ten years of feeding families and knowing what actually matters to people, a compulsion to build things that serve someone other than the demo, and enough stubbornness to see them through to production.

The agents handle what agents handle well. I handle what still requires a human: the judgment calls, the vision, the question of whether a thing should exist at all.
