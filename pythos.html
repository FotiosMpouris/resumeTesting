The Poor People App

The Problem — In Numbers

The social safety net is running on outdated software. The numbers don't lie:

  • 65% of middle-class Americans are struggling financially
  • Groceries cost 20% more than three years ago; consumer prices up 24% since the pandemic
  • 47% of U.S. households struggle to afford basic necessities
  • Administrative overhead consumes 15–25% of all U.S. health spending — up to $1 trillion per year
  • Social Security disability decisions average 220–240 days; ~60–67% denied on first application
  • Americans spend 13 hours per year just preparing a tax return

The wealthy have always had access to sophisticated tools — IRS codes, corporate structures, attorneys, accountants, and private networks. The Poor People App gives everyone else those same tools, powered by AI, blockchain, and decentralized coordination software.

The Model — Uber for Human Services

PPA connects people who need help with people who can provide it — cutting bureaucratic overhead out of the equation entirely:

  • Families drowning in childcare costs ↔ vetted local caregivers (avg. U.S. cost: $13,128/yr per child)
  • Seniors abandoned by the system ↔ vetted companions (professional elder care: $5,000+/month)
  • Students without tutors ↔ retired teachers and college students ($25–$100/hour private rate)
  • People buried in bureaucracy ↔ experienced navigators (SSA, Medicare, tax prep)

No income verification. No waiting lists. No humiliation. Reputation-based matching where the best providers surface organically.

The Sentinel Program

AI handles the routine 95% of cases. The Sentinel Program bridges efficiency with human judgment for the rest. Recruited from retired military, intelligence professionals, bankers, first responders, and financial advisors — Sentinels review edge cases that require real-world judgment: vetting caregivers, flagging anomalies, serving as community liaisons. Traditional social services spend 25% of their budget on administrative overhead. Sentinels handle the 5% that actually needs human eyes.

Decentralized by Design — Nostr & Bitcoin

PPA must be able to fail while the community survives. If the company dissolves, Nostr identities, reputation, and connections persist — because they live on a protocol, not a platform. Every user identity is a cryptographic keypair they control. Service listings, reviews, and reputation are signed events portable across relays. Bitcoin backs the platform treasury as an inflation hedge. Lightning Network handles instant micro-payments. The Poor People Coin (PPC) — an ERC-20 utility token — rewards verified participation and governs platform direction.

The Mobilization Agent — Live & Operational

The first AI agent in the PPA architecture is already running. The Mobilization Agent is a GPT-4o-powered content engine with a specific writer's persona baked in: George Orwell's clarity + George Washington's resolve + Odysseus' strategic mind. It doesn't pitch. It teaches people how to master the system.

It generates five content types — Text Posts, Sora Video Scripts (with auto-generated TTS audio narration), GIF Prompts, Direct Messages, and Email Outreach — all grounded in a live knowledge base of research documents that updates automatically as new material is added to S3. No code changes required.

Generated content deploys live with one click to Telegram and Nostr relays. The agent also runs a self-assessment after every generation — scoring its own knowledge base usage and specificity on a 0–10 scale, storing results in a learning memory that improves future output over time.

Tech: Next.js 15 · TypeScript · React 18 · AWS Lambda (Python) · DynamoDB · S3 · OpenAI GPT-4o · OpenAI TTS-1-HD · Telegram Bot API · Nostr Protocol · Docker · Nginx · Let's Encrypt
