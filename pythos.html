The Poor People App

The social safety net is running on outdated software. While the middle class gets squeezed by inflation and AI threatens entry-level jobs, the existing system offers nothing but bureaucratic runarounds and programs you don't qualify for. We're building the alternative.

PPA is economic infrastructure for regular people. This isn't about charity—it's about leverage. We're creating a platform where communities organize collectively, where your data becomes an asset you control, and where transparent technology replaces inefficient bureaucracy. Think Uber's playbook applied to human services: connecting people who need help with people who can provide it, powered by AI agents that handle the complexity while keeping humans in control.

The long-term vision includes tokenized incentives through Poor People Coin (PPC), Bitcoin-based inflation protection, and a network powerful enough that politicians can't ignore it. But right now? We're in the building phase—proving there's demand, assembling the team, and doing this the right way with full legal compliance.

This is economic mobilization disguised as an app. The first supporters aren't just users—they're founding members of a movement to upgrade how America takes care of its own.

WHY BITCOIN

Sound Money for an Uncertain Future

Your paycheck buys less every month. The government prints trillions, inflation steals your purchasing power, and traditional savings accounts get destroyed by monetary policy you have no control over. Bitcoin is the hedge.

Censorship-Resistant Infrastructure

Banks freeze accounts. Payment processors deplatform organizations. Centralized systems have single points of failure controlled by people who don't answer to you. Bitcoin is permissionless—no one can stop you from holding it, sending it, or building on top of it. For a platform serving people the system already ignores, that's not a luxury. It's a necessity.

Bitcoin's Role in PPA

We're exploring Bitcoin as the foundation for PPA's treasury reserves. As platform revenue grows, we accumulate Bitcoin—not because we're gambling on price increases, but because it's the only asset governments can't print more of when it's politically convenient. When inflation hits and service costs spike, a Bitcoin-backed treasury means PPA can keep operating while traditional organizations scramble for emergency funding.

This isn't about getting rich. It's about building something that can't be shut down when we become inconvenient. The middle class needs infrastructure that works when traditional systems fail. Bitcoin makes that possible.
