Origin Story

Nobody builds four production AI systems by following a roadmap.

Ten years running The Grillin Greek — not managing it, building it. The kind of operation where the system breaks at 7pm on a Saturday with a full house and you either solve it in real time or you don’t. That’s what operations actually means. No diagram captures it.

Then art school, for the discipline of honest observation. Then five years inside nonprofit organizations — close enough to institutional care to watch, with real frustration, how resources meant for people get absorbed into the machinery of maintaining the institution itself.

Then Python. Then the models arrived and the whole game changed overnight.

No CS degree. What’s here instead: a bias toward production over prototype, a tolerance for systems that actually touch people’s lives, and a long-term commitment to building the harness — the infrastructure, agents, and coordination layers that let frontier AI do useful work for people who aren’t already developers. Because the models will get smarter. The question is whether the scaffolding is ready when they do.
