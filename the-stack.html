Background

Restaurant first — ten years feeding people, understanding what people actually need versus what they ask for. Then art school. Then nearly five years working inside nonprofit organizations in various roles — programs, operations, community coordination — long enough to understand how institutional care breaks down, where resources disappear, and what it costs people when systems prioritize their own survival over the people they serve.

Then Unity. Then Python. Then the agents arrived and everything compressed.

I don't have a CS degree. What I have is a compulsion to build things that serve someone other than the demo, and enough stubbornness to see them through to production. Hard-E started as a favor. OpenCare started because my family needed it. Every project below is either used by a real client, my own family, or running on a server right now.

The stack changes constantly. This is what I'm working in today.
